How to read this radar

Everyone is scored against your project out of 100 points across the five things you selected. The more of those five you share, the closer they sit to the centre. Anything under 20% is filtered out entirely.

Where the 100 points come from

Project type25
Tools shared25
Objective20
Team18
Business unit12

Project type, objective and unit are all-or-nothing — you match or you don’t. Tools scale with overlap, so 4 of your 5 tools in common scores far higher than 1. Team only counts if you’re in the same unit to begin with. Someone in a different unit doing the exact same work with the same stack still tops out at 70% — which is the point: that’s the person you’d never have found otherwise.

What your score means — and what to do

80–100%
Overlap risk
Same project type, same objective, same tools. You are building the same thing twice. Meet this week — merge scope or split ownership.
60–79%
Co-build
Same destination, different route. Worth a shared roadmap and reusing each other’s components rather than writing them twice.
40–59%
Share the playbook
Different project, same toolkit or same category. Trade templates, gotchas and vendor lessons — you’ll each save a sprint.
20–39%
Network & skill-share
Loosely adjacent — usually one shared signal. A 20-minute intro; keep each other on the radar for later.
<20%
Not shown
Below the noise floor. Hidden so the radar stays readable.
